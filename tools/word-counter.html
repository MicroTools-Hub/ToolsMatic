Understanding Readability Scores & Metrics

üéØ Flesch Reading Ease (0-100)

90-100: Very Easy (5th grade, children's books). 80-89: Easy (6th grade, newspapers). 70-79: Fairly Easy (7th grade). 60-69: Standard (8th-9th grade, most popular web content). 50-59: Fairly Difficult (10-12th grade). 30-49: Difficult (College-level). 0-29: Very Difficult (Professional/Academic). Most successful blogs target 60-70 for broad accessibility.

üìä Flesch-Kincaid Grade Level

Indicates the U.S. school grade level needed to understand the text. Grade 8 is ideal for general audiences. Grade 6 is great for broad accessibility. Grades 10-12 suit well-educated audiences. Grades 13-16+ indicate college/professional level. News typically targets grade 8-10 for mass appeal.

üìà Gunning Fog Index

Another readability metric based on sentence length and complex words (3+ syllables). Higher scores = more difficult text. Typically ranges from 6-17. Gunning Fog Score of 12 equals 12th grade level. Use alongside Flesch-Kincaid for comprehensive analysis.

üî¢ Coleman-Liau Index

Readability metric based on characters instead of syllables. Useful for short texts where syllable counting is unreliable. Also produces U.S. grade level. Typically ranges from 0-18. Useful cross-reference with other metrics.

How to Improve Your Text's Readability

Use the word counter to identify areas for improvement, then make adjustments and watch the metrics update in real-time:

‚úÇÔ∏è Shorten Sentences

Break long, complex sentences into shorter ones (12-15 words average). Shorter sentences = lower Flesch-Kincaid grade and higher Flesch ease score. Each sentence should contain one main idea.

üî§ Choose Simpler Words

Replace complex vocabulary with common alternatives. Instead of "utilize," use "use". Instead of "ameliorate," use "improve". Simpler words reduce syllable count and improve readability scores significantly.

üìÑ Break Paragraphs

Split dense paragraphs into shorter ones (3-5 sentences). Shorter paragraphs increase white space, making text feel less overwhelming and easier to read on screens.

‚ùå Reduce Syllables per Word

Use short words (1-2 syllables) instead of long ones (4+ syllables). This directly improves all readability scores. The tool shows your average syllables/word‚Äîtarget below 1.6.

üéØ Vary Sentence Structure

Mix short sentences with occasional longer ones. Prevents monotony. Include simple sentences, complex sentences, and compound sentences for natural rhythm.

üîë Control Keyword Repetition

Use the keyword cloud to spot overused words. While keyword repetition helps SEO, excessive repetition hurts readability. Target 1-2% keyword density (keyword frequency √∑ total words).
